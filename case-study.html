Discount Compiler – Chrome Extension for Automatic Discounts

A portfolio case study demonstrating product management skills, from user research to MVP and A/B testing.

Problem Statement

Frequent online shoppers, particularly college students, often miss savings because discounts are scattered across sites or hidden behind logins. Student discounts require extra verification via Student Beans or Unidays, causing friction and lost savings.

  • Time-consuming coupon searches
  • Student discounts locked behind logins
  • Frustration leading to abandoned discounts

User Research & Insights

Hypothesis: Students are willing to verify once, but don’t want to leave checkout repeatedly.

  • Surveys and interviews on student shopping habits
  • Competitive analysis of Honey, Unidays, Student Beans, and Capital One Shopping

Market & Competitive Analysis

Competitor Strengths Weaknesses Opportunity
Honey Large user base, auto-applies coupons Not student-focused Combine automation + student-specific savings
Unidays Exclusive student discounts Requires switching platforms Integrate with checkout for frictionless experience
Student Beans Strong student verification & partnerships Discounts not automated at checkout One-click auto-apply using Student Beans API
Capital One Shopping Cashback + coupons, broad coverage Not student-focused Lightweight, student-first solution

Solution Concept

Product Vision: Save students time and money by automatically applying the best discounts at checkout.

MVP Features:

  • Auto-detect coupon codes and apply at checkout
  • Student verification (.edu email)
  • Savings preview in extension popup

Future Features:

  • Personalized recommendations based on past purchases
  • Cashback / points system
  • Integration with additional e-commerce platforms

A/B Testing & Experimentation

Hypothesis: Showing savings information increases discount application.

  • Control: Discounts only
  • Variant A: Discounts + total savings
  • Variant B: Discounts + savings + recommended deals

Metrics: % of users clicking “Apply All”, average savings per user, retention

Success Metrics

  • Activation: % applying a discount on first use
  • Retention: % returning weekly
  • Engagement: Avg discounts applied per month
  • Impact: Total $ saved per user

Reflection

This case study demonstrates:

  • Identification of a specific user pain point
  • Competitor evaluation and gap analysis
  • Designing a solution with clear MVP and roadmap
  • Data-driven decision making through A/B testing
  • Consideration of metrics and measurable outcomes, balancing user experience with business impact
